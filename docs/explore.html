Catalonia liveability

mitma-sedona · geo-browser

Where could you live well in Catalonia — near a frequent train, a climbing gym or yoga nearby, a hospital in reach, green and coast close, clean air, and away from heavy industry and motorway noise? This map carries an H3 res-8 multi-criteria liveability index over 45,220 hexagons, built as an applied spatial-data-science pipeline on Apache Sedona. Every dimension is wired to real data — MITMA mobility, Renfe Rodalies + FGC GTFS, OpenStreetMap amenities, EEA tree cover, Natura 2000, XVPCA air quality, Landsat heat and VIIRS night-light — and every number is reproducible from the repository.

The liveability score is a relative index — a starting question, not a guarantee, not a recommendation, not an operational service. Distances are computed in metres (EPSG:25831); the mobility signal is a 7-day March-2024 window.

    Scope & caveats

    • Relative index, not a guarantee. The score orders hexes within Catalonia; it makes no absolute or "you should move here" claim.
    • Dev-scope window. The mobility signal is 7 days of March 2024 (2024-03-04 … 10), not a full year — a representative slice, not a seasonal average.
    • Sparse amenity layers. Some proximity layers cover only part of the grid — climbing ~49%, hospitals ~31%, yoga ~15%, coast ~6%. Cells with no amenity in the 10 km catchment simply earn no closeness reward there (and a recoloured presence metric renders them grey) — that absence is itself the finding, not a penalty.
    • Null is a distinct state. A missing value is rendered slate-grey, never as a ramp-low or zero value.

    How it's scored — dimensions & sources

    The liveability score sums six weighted dimensions over each H3 cell. Amenity closeness (climb, yoga, hospital, green) is a saturating positive reward — full bonus at the doorstep, decaying to zero at a 10 km catchment edge — so present-but-far always beats absent. Genuine harms (air, heat, light, industry, motorway) stay negative. All distances are computed in metres (EPSG:25831). Each source below is tagged live (wired to real data and feeding the gold table) or planned (a refinement not yet fed in). Default weights from configs/weights.yaml.
